What is Propolis?

Propolis is a substance used by bees to protect their hive from impurities. The ancient Greeks were familiar with propolis, and the name is thought to derive from their language: 'pro polis' meaning "before the city." Propolis consists mainly of resins and wax from tree buds, as well as pine and spruce resin. Its exact composition can vary depending on the bees' location and the plants they access.

Harvested with Care

The bees gather the necessary ingredients during their foraging, transforming them into propolis, which is then spread around the hive. A bee colony only collects 80–100 grams of propolis each season to maintain the hive's balance. To ensure product quality, Forever uses special tools for collecting the propolis.
